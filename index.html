Common questions

Things engineers always ask.

What happens when the engagement ends? Do we own the code?
Yes, fully. You receive the entire Git repository, all credentials are rotated to your team, and the final session is a walkthrough with your engineers. The repository contains the runbook — there's no separate documentation that goes stale. No source-code escrow nonsense, no per-seat licensing.
What if my team can't maintain it after handoff?
The code is written for readability, not cleverness — every PowerShell module includes inline comments explaining why, not just what. An optional support retainer is available (typically 4–8 hrs/month) for periodic reviews, version bumps, and "we want to extend this" requests. Most clients use it for the first quarter, then drop to ad-hoc.
Do you sign NDAs and how do you handle credentials?
NDAs are signed by default — yours or mine, whichever your legal prefers. Credentials are handled via short-lived service principals or Azure Key Vault references; no production passwords are ever committed to Git or stored locally. All work is GDPR-compliant and EU-resident.
How quickly can you start a project?
Typically within 2 weeks of the proposal being signed. The free 30-minute audit can usually be scheduled within 3 business days. For urgent stabilization work — a broken patch cycle, a failed compliance audit — same-week starts are sometimes possible, just ask.
Do you only work with the Microsoft stack?
That's the deepest specialty — Azure, Entra ID, Intune, PowerShell, MSIX. XenServer and Active Directory are also home turf. For projects that touch Linux, AWS, or hybrid setups, I'm comfortable but will be honest about what's a strength versus what's a learning curve. If something is outside scope, I'll say so on the discovery call.
Why fixed-scope quotes instead of an hourly retainer?
Because retainers reward slowness. Fixed scope means I'm motivated to ship, you know what you're paying, and your finance team gets a clean PO. The hourly rate (70–100€) only applies to the discovery and ad-hoc support tier; everything build-related is quoted as a deliverable.
